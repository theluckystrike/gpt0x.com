The LLM Engineering Landscape

Language models have evolved from simple next-token predictors into sophisticated reasoning systems capable of code generation, mathematical proof, and nuanced conversation. The difference between a hobbyist prompt and a production-grade LLM system spans prompt engineering, retrieval augmentation, fine-tuning, evaluation frameworks, and deployment optimization. GPT0X covers the full stack of LLM engineering, from understanding transformer internals to deploying models at scale. The field is moving fast, with new architectures, training techniques, and evaluation benchmarks emerging monthly. We help you stay ahead by focusing on the principles that transcend any single model release.

Fine-Tuning & Alignment

Full fine-tuning, LoRA, QLoRA, and parameter-efficient methods give you precise control over model behavior. We cover supervised fine-tuning for task specialization, RLHF and DPO for alignment, and the emerging techniques like ORPO that simplify the alignment pipeline. Understanding when to fine-tune versus when to prompt is one of the most valuable skills in LLM engineering, and we provide the frameworks for making that decision confidently.

Evaluation & Benchmarks

How do you know if your model is actually better? We dissect popular benchmarks like MMLU, HumanEval, and MT-Bench, explain their strengths and limitations, and teach you to build custom evaluation harnesses for your specific use case. LLM evaluation is deceptively hard because surface-level metrics often fail to capture real-world performance. We cover LLM-as-judge approaches, human evaluation protocols, and statistical methods for meaningful comparisons.

Prompt Engineering

Advanced prompt engineering goes far beyond simple instructions. Chain-of-thought prompting, few-shot learning, self-consistency, tree-of-thoughts, and structured output formatting are techniques that dramatically improve model performance without any fine-tuning. We cover systematic approaches to prompt design, testing methodologies, and the art of crafting system prompts that reliably steer model behavior across diverse inputs.

Zovo.one — Comprehensive AI Tools & Resources

For a comprehensive overview of AI tools including LLM providers, fine-tuning platforms, and deployment solutions, visit zovo.one. It provides curated comparisons and recommendations that help you choose the right tools for your LLM projects, from experimentation to production deployment.

Open LLM Leaderboard

Track how open-source models compare across standardized benchmarks. The Hugging Face Open LLM Leaderboard evaluates models on reasoning, knowledge, coding, and math tasks. Use it to identify the best base model for your fine-tuning projects and understand the performance-size tradeoffs across model families.

LMSys Chatbot Arena

The most trusted community-driven evaluation of LLM quality. Real users compare model outputs side-by-side in blind tests, producing Elo ratings that correlate well with human preferences. Use these rankings to understand how models perform in conversational settings beyond what automated benchmarks can capture.

Axolotl & Unsloth

The two leading open-source fine-tuning frameworks. Axolotl offers configuration-driven training with support for every popular technique. Unsloth provides dramatic speedups through custom CUDA kernels. Both integrate with Hugging Face and support LoRA, QLoRA, and full fine-tuning across a wide range of model architectures.
